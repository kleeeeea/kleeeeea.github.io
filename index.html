About

Keqian Li is now at Meta's AI Applied Research team. Before Meta, he was a Researcher at Yahoo Research affiliated with Verizon. He obtained his Ph.D. from The College of Engineering at UC Santa Barbara, advised by Prof. Xifeng Yan, his B.Eng in Tsinghua Univeristy from the Special Pilot CS Class program advised by Prof. Andrew Chi-Chih Yao, and his B.Econ in SEM, Tsinghua Univeristy.

Research








Concept Mining

Concept mining is an activity that results in the extraction of concepts from artifacts. Solutions to the task typically involve aspects of artificial intelligence and statistics such as data mining and text mining and across different domain, including both structured relational data and unstructured natural language.

Concept Representation Learning

Concept representation learning leverage task-aware interpretable representation of concepts for end to end learnings tasks in semi-structured and structured domain, including technical documents, relation table, and heterogeneous data lake on Hadoop.

Distributed Auto ML

The ever-growing computation capability distributed infrastructure brings unique and non-trivial challenges for traditional Auto-ML, including the explosion of data dimensions, the expected absence of features, and the heterogeneity of information. Distributed Auto ML aims to overcome the sheer scale of the computation and overhead and relieve engineers and scientists from constantly engaging in the ML life-cycle by automating the extraction, loading, transformation and optimization in an end to end fashion.

Data Science for Social Good

I also participated in prominent data science research and technology that arise in diverse fields that involves the use of scientific methods, processes, algorithms and systems to extract knowledge and insights from noisy, structured and unstructured data, and to apply knowledge and actionable insights from data across a broad range of application domains including computational social science, social media moderation, recommender systems and information visualization.
